The Rochester Curriculum requires students to complete a cluster in two out of three academic divisions: Humanities, Social Sciences, and Natural Sciences and Engineering. Here are the key details about how the cluster requirement works:

1. **Cluster Composition**: Each cluster consists of thematically related courses, requiring a minimum of twelve credits, which is typically equivalent to three courses.

2. **Divisional Structure**: Students must choose clusters from the two divisions they are not majoring in. For example, if a student is majoring in Natural Sciences and Engineering, they need to complete clusters in both Humanities and Social Sciences.

3. **Course Restrictions**: Students cannot use the same course to fulfill the requirements of two different clusters. Additionally, students are not allowed to use the Ethics and Values Cluster from the Philosophy department if they plan to use the Logic Cluster from the same department to fulfill Natural Sciences requirements.

4. **Cluster Policies**: The average grade within the cluster must be C or better to meet the requirements.

5. **Course Work Location**: Typically, only courses taken at the University of Rochester can count toward cluster requirements. However, courses taken during a study abroad program may count if approved by the department offering the cluster.

For more details, you can visit the [Cluster Policies page](https://rochester.edu/college/ccas/undergraduate/curriculum/clusters.html).

To get an independent study course approved and registered, you need to follow these steps:

1. **Plan the Course**: Work with a full-time faculty instructor who will guide you and evaluate your work at the end of the semester.
   
2. **Develop a Proposal**: Create a written proposal for your independent study, typically 2-3 pages long, which may include elements like a bibliography. It's advisable to consult with your faculty advisor on what to include in the proposal.

3. **Obtain Approval**: Your completed proposal and the online Independent Study Form must be reviewed and approved by:
   - The faculty advisor
   - The chair of the department
   - The appropriate Dean (Senior Associate Dean of Academic and Student Affairs for undergraduates, Associate Dean of Graduate Studies for graduate students)

4. **Submit the Independent Study Form**: You must submit the approved Independent Study Form. Confirmation of approval is indicated by receiving your copy of the form by mail.

5. **Registration**: The actual registration will happen under the appropriate subject heading and numbered course (e.g., 290 for undergraduate courses). Regular tuition charges apply.

You can add independent study courses until the end of the third full week of classes, but it's important to leave enough time for the approval process and to substitute another course if needed. For more information, you can visit [the University of Rochester Registrar's page](https://rochester.edu/registrar/students/registration/undergraduate.html).
